Which Dogs Are Hypoallergenic? The Truth

Many individuals believe that family pet allergic reactions are brought on by a cat's or dog's fur, however the genuine source of family pet allergic reactions is frequently a protein that remains in the urine and saliva of cats and dogs. You can find this type of clinging to a dog's dried flaky skin.

Some pet types are marketed as hypoallergenic since they do not shed fur as much as others or they shed extremely little. The dander that causes allergies isn't released in the air or on the ground like other dogs due to the fact that they do not shed as much. While you might have less dog hair with a with a dog that does not shed, no dog type is hypoallergenic.

How to reduce allergy symptoms around dogs

If you are allergic to being around dogs but still want to get one, follow these steps to reduce the risk of triggering your allergies.

  • Try to choose a small dog. A small dog will shed less.
  • Make sure your dog do not spend much time in the room you spend most of your time
  • If you have great weather, let your dog play outside often with supervision.
  • Choose flooring that does not require carpets. Dander will get trapped in carpets.
  • Give your dog a bathe weekly to remove dander.
  • Use an air purifier.

Mixed Breed Dogs and Hybrid Dogs

Mixed breed and hybrid dogs are those that have been crossbred with other breeds of dogs. Say for instance a rottweiler is bred with a pitbull, those offsprings will be known as mixed breeds or hybrid dogs. When dogs are mixed they will most likely have the attributes of both parents. Some will be able to mix a shedding dog with a non shedding dog breed to create a crossbreed that is half shedding and half non shedding dog.
